Explore Delhi's Monuments and Travel Places

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the tallest brick minarets in the world. It stands at 73 meters tall and is a perfect example of Indo-Islamic Afghan architecture.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is an iconic symbol of India's history and the seat of the Mughal Empire. It is made of red sandstone and hosts the famous sound and light show in the evenings.

India Gate

India Gate is a war memorial dedicated to soldiers who died during World War I. Located in the heart of the city, it is one of Delhi's most famous landmarks.

Humayun's Tomb

Humayun's Tomb is a beautiful example of Mughal architecture and a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is the tomb of the Mughal Emperor Humayun and is the inspiration for the Taj Mahal.

Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple is known for its lotus-shaped structure and is the central house of worship for the Bahá'í Faith. It is open to people of all religions.

Akshardham Temple

Akshardham Temple is an architectural marvel showcasing India's cultural heritage. It features intricate carvings, exhibitions, and a stunning water show.
